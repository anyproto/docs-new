# Vault

### What is a Vault?

A **Vault** is your personal, encrypted container in Anytype. Everything you create — notes, tasks, projects, media — lives inside your Vault, stored directly on your device.

If you're coming from other apps, think of your Vault as your account, except that no company holds the keys. Your Vault is protected by cryptography so strong that all the computers on Earth working together couldn't break into it.

### Why it matters

Most apps store your data on their servers, and you trust them to keep it safe. With Anytype, your Vault is encrypted on your device before anything is synced. You own your data, and only you (or people you choose to share with) can read it.

This means your information stays private by default — not because of a company's privacy policy, but because of math.

### How it works

Your Vault is created the first time you set up Anytype. Along with it, you receive a **Key** — a 12-word phrase that is the only way to access your Vault. Think of it like a master password that can never be changed or reset.

{% hint style="warning" %}
If you lose your Key and are logged out on all devices, there is no way to recover your Vault. Save your Key somewhere safe. See [Key](key.md) for details.
{% endhint %}

Inside your Vault, you create **Channels** — separate workspaces for different areas of your life or work. Your personal notes might live in one Channel, while a shared team workspace lives in another. All Channels live inside your single Vault.

{% hint style="info" %}
Your Vault syncs across your devices automatically when you're online. You can also work fully offline — changes sync the next time you connect.
{% endhint %}
