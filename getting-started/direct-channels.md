# Direct Channels

### What is a Direct Channel?

A **Direct Channel** is a private, one-on-one conversation between you and another member. Unlike regular Channels — which can hold notes, tasks, multiple chats, and many members — a Direct Channel is just for two people.

Both participants have equal access. There's no admin, no role hierarchy, no permissions to set. It's a place for quick check-ins, a private discussion, sharing a single Object, or a side conversation about something happening in another Channel.

### Why it matters

In a multi-member Channel, every conversation is visible to everyone. Sometimes you need a side channel — a quick question to one teammate, a private update, a personal note. Direct Channels give you that without creating a whole new shared Channel just for the two of you.

They're lightweight by design: no settings to configure, no widgets to set up. You start talking, the channel exists.

### How it works

A Direct Channel is created automatically when you start a conversation with another member. Once created:

- Both members see the Direct Channel in their **Vault sidebar**
- Either member can post messages, share Objects, react, or pin messages
- Either member can leave (which removes their side of the channel)
- Both can mute notifications independently

You can have a Direct Channel with any other member — including members of shared Channels you both belong to. The Direct Channel exists separately from those shared Channels.

### Starting a Direct Channel

There are two ways to start one:

**From a member's profile:**

1. Click any member's profile picture or name (in a Chat, in a Channel members list, or in their @-mention).
2. Click **Send message** in the profile popup.
3. The Direct Channel opens — type your first message and send.

**From the Vault:**

1. Open the Vault sidebar (the leftmost panel).
2. Click the **+** button at the top of the Vault.
3. Choose **New Direct Channel**.
4. Search for the member by name and select them.

### What you can do in a Direct Channel

A Direct Channel works like a Chat — you have access to all the same features:

- Send and react to messages
- Share Objects (drag in from anywhere or use @-mention)
- Upload files and images
- Pin messages to the top of the conversation
- Search messages with Cmd/Ctrl + F
- Edit and delete your own messages
- Configure per-channel notifications

Since both members are equal, there's no concept of Owner-only actions inside a Direct Channel.

### Notifications

By default, you'll be notified about every message in a Direct Channel. To change this:

1. Right-click the Direct Channel in the Vault, **or**
2. Click the three-dot menu inside the channel.
3. Choose **Enable all**, **Mentions only**, or **Disable all**.

Each member sets their own notification preferences for the same Direct Channel.

### Leaving a Direct Channel

You can leave a Direct Channel by:

1. Right-clicking it in the Vault.
2. Selecting **Leave Direct Channel**.

When you leave, the channel disappears from your Vault — but the other member's side remains. They'll see that you've left, and any new messages they send won't reach you. If you want to reconnect, you can start a new Direct Channel with them.

To leave permanently and clear all history, you can also delete the channel — but this only deletes your local copy. The other member retains their side.

### Tips

{% hint style="info" %}
**Direct Channels are not encrypted by sender — they use the same encryption as the rest of Anytype.** Messages are end-to-end encrypted between the two participants and synced through the network the same way Channel messages are.
{% endhint %}

{% hint style="info" %}
**Use Direct Channels for cross-Channel conversations.** If you and a teammate both belong to several shared Channels, a Direct Channel gives you a single place to talk about whatever — instead of fragmenting the conversation across the Chats of each shared Channel.
{% endhint %}

{% hint style="warning" %}
You can't add a third person to a Direct Channel. If a one-on-one conversation needs to expand, create a regular Channel and invite both people.
{% endhint %}
